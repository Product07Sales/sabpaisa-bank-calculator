How It Works

Two Perspectives. One Seamless Experience.

Split Settlement works differently for merchants and customers — but both get a frictionless experience.

🏢

For the Merchant

Automated splitting replaces manual reconciliation and bank transfers

  • 1 One-time setup — Ops team configures your split rules on the dashboard
  • 2 Automatic processing — Every settlement is split per your configured rules
  • 3 Transparent reporting — Full visibility into how each payment was split
  • 4 NEFT tracking — Track every transfer from queued to confirmed
  • 5 Zero integration changes — Works with your existing payment setup
👤

For the Customer

Nothing changes. The customer pays a single amount — the split happens behind the scenes.

  • 1 Sees single amount — e.g., Rs 10,000 as total admission fee
  • 2 Pays via any method — UPI, Net Banking, Cards, Wallets
  • 3 Gets confirmation — Single receipt for the full payment
  • 4 Split is invisible — Distribution happens entirely on the backend
  • 5 Seamless experience — No extra steps, no confusion

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about Split Settlement.

Does the customer see the split?

No. The customer pays a single amount. Splitting happens entirely on the backend during settlement. The customer receives a single receipt for the full payment.

Can split rules be changed after setup?

Yes. Split configurations can be updated anytime by the ops team. Changes apply to future settlements — existing settlements are not affected.

What happens if a split transfer fails?

The failed transfer is flagged in the system. The ops team can investigate the failure reason and retry or resolve the issue through the dashboard.

Can a merchant have different splits for different transactions?

Yes. With Dynamic Account or Institute-Based patterns, each transaction can specify different recipients and amounts via UDF fields.

Are there limits on the number of beneficiaries?

No hard limit. Multiple beneficiaries can be configured with priority ordering. Each can have independent amount rules and account configurations.

Does the merchant need to change their payment integration?

No, as long as the merchant is already sending the required data in their UDF (User Defined Fields) during payment. No new integration work is needed.

How are PG fees handled in a split?

Two options: (1) Prorate fees proportionally across all beneficiaries, or (2) Deduct entirely from the primary beneficiary. This is configurable per merchant.

What payment methods are supported?

All methods supported by the payment gateway — UPI, Net Banking, Credit/Debit Cards, Wallets. The split logic is payment-method agnostic.
